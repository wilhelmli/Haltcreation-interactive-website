EXTROVERTED INTROVERT

Being social awkward is not necessarily a bad trait. It is powerful characteristic to focus on myself rather than constantly being distracted by what other people are doing. 

1. Meet the real me

The first impression of me to other people is usually outgoing and active. However, the truth is always more to it than what it seems. I have always been a sensitive person, meaning that I am person with anxiety and I have a hard time letting go.

Growing up in China, I never had a lot of friends. Rather than being upset that I sometimes ate my lunch alone, I was curious about the social dynamics around me. It seemed to be a world that I would never understand.

I was never taught how to make friends. I did not understand why people are always in groups. A large factor of me being alone is that I was not making the initiative to hangout with other people.

I was simply just fine by myself. 

2. Ways to cope with it

As I grew older, I came to the realization that it is an absolute social advantage to be an outgoing person in any social situation. 

Although I was used to being alone, I was losing the opportunity for more potential growth from social interactions. Lack of exposure to other minds limited my vision and understanding towards things. Rather than sometimes being challenged by other ideas, I was more or less close minded because of lack of social interactions.

Finding the answer

Growing up I have always loved reading, but for leisure.

Towards the end of my second year of university, I was hungry for personal growth. Although I overcame some challenges in social interactions, it did little to help me to shape my reality. I asked myself simple but complicated questions like:


What do I want to do?
What friends are for?
Who should I become?
As if I was uncapable of human emotions.


Therefore, I came to realization that I needed to read more, but this time, for growth:

When frustrated with rare success in relationships, I went for books on dating advices, like THE GAME by Neil Strauss.

When struggled with awkward and boring conversations, I went for books like how How to Win Friends and Influence People by Dale Carnegie.

Click here to my booklist

It was the spark of having a great reading routine. I  found more comfort in reading than seeking advices from others. As an introvert, it is a sensitive topic to share my thoughts with others. Rather than directing the issues to others, I was be able find the problems from myself first, which is usually very important in situations with tensions.




July 31st, 2019
Wilhelm from Victoria, Canada
