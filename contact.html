< Philosophy

A lot of people may ask, how do art and creative stuff relate to my degree in economics. I have always like to have a couple side projects growing up.

While I was in University, I saw side projects as an escape to unplug myself from stressful school work.
It doesn’t matter to me who I show my work to, at the beginning it was just a bunch of tiny bits and pieces I thought of in class when zoning out in class.

And it doesn’t matter if I have been wanting to put something together. I always strive to scratch my own itch, creating something that truly represents what is mine.

Excercise is part of my daily routine.

I enjoy traveling and learning.

my skill sets

I love tech. My main tools are the Adobe illustrator, Photoshop, MacOS, Figure (by Propellerhead), Logic Pro X, Final Cut Pro, R-Studio, and Snapseed.

Unlike the conventional process of working on projects, I edit most of my work on my iPhone (catalogs).
In the age of Social Media, there is an ever increasing demand for quick, screen-ready contents.

Our smartphones are some powerful, convenient device, which is perfect for creative work, on demand.

my drive

From the beginning of my journey abroad, I’ve been trying to fit in, understanding what other individuals or societies think it is the best.
However, I have came to the conclusion that following the mainstream does not only make me feel lost but also losing focus on what I HAVE but others don’t.

I want to share, to discuss my ideas and strategies with as many people as possible. I love failures, and I treasure lessons. Afterall, they are just a combination factors of success. Right?

< Vision

While pursuing an economics at University of Victoria, I worked on multiple projects at my spare time. As I was about to graduate half a year early, I planned an art show over the summer before the fall term.

The art show was intended to showcase the creative work I have done through HALT CREATION.

The theme of the art show was Create Your Own Reality, I created a space where people can come in and experience my concept of school, how I view my relationship with the education system.

The sculptures I created in particular, such as the giant calculator, giant pencil and giant eraser, symbolized the enormous impact, of which education has on me:

day by day I was feeling INCREDIBLY SMALL upon graduation, because the world suddenly opened up to me,
and there was little I already knew.
