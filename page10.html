What I think happened

I have adapted the idea that with the conveniency of technology and transportation, it becomes possible to live remotely, or in some cases, more enjoyable and economically more affordable. I have came across cases like someone will be working in San Fransisco, where one can get much higher pay, but raising a family on Vancouver island, where social welfare is better and living cost is relatively more affordable .
Overtime, traveling developed into not only a reliable source of new perspectives but also a catalyst for my personal growth. 

China

I grew up in a city called Zhanjiang in the southwest end of China. In history, it was called Guangzhouwan, as it is southwest city of Guangzhou on an inlet of the South China Sea. The region was still a small fishing port when it was occupied by the French in 1898. 

In a harbour near my hometown city, a giant cruise ship called “Sea City”

My rugby team in high school (2016)

Canada

At the age of 16, I got sent off to Canada for high school. I settled down at a country town called Abbotsford, about an hour drive from Vancouver. It was a challenging year at the beginning. I have stayed in multiple homestays during that period of time. Some good experiences, some bad ones, even my brother David, who also came to Canada a year after me, got into weird shit with his homestays that could not be explained, but finally settled down with a nice christian family. A mix of hormones and fit drove my mental health to the bottom. I spent most of my time dreaming, gaming in my basement, with rare success in school work and relationships.

Then, my mental health took a sharp turn as one day I sold all my gaming devices, and decided to step out of the basement. Coming from the part of world where humidity can soak through the walls, I started training for rugby everyday after school in the cold. Although I was never good at it, I was pleased with the shift of my physical appearance as I got stronger and became more confident.

As an international student, I went on countless trips to Downtown Victoria and Butchart Gardens with school. It is a nice town but I never thought I’d ever eventually go study Economics on the island. Despite the fact I was always weak at academics, I got accepted to most of the universities I picked. However, I chose University of Victoria for its much competitive cost.

In front of Orangerie, sport is indeed the best way to make friends. (2017)

Germany

The spark of my interest in the European culture started in my first year of university. I took elementary german courses as electives for fun. Towards the end of my freshman year, I had the urge to travel somewhere for the summer. It was around that period of time, I learnt about the program CSSG in class, where students go on a 6-week journey during the summer, staying in homestays, only German speaking allowed, in some small town called Kassel in Germany.

Sounds familiar?

Surrounded by other students from different universities across Canada, I started a 4-year adventure peeking into different culture through language learning. It was an exchange experience within an exchange experience, and so the story goes.

Maybe that is what my life is set out to be 6 years ago. Settling down may never be a lifestyle I would pursue.




Aug 13th, 2019
Wilhelm from Victoria, Canada
