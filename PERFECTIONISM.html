PERFECTIONISM

While perfection is often desired, it is a dangerous behaviour that restricts one's productivity.

1. What I think happened

These are the WOW moments in life. Thoughtful designs are satisfying for both creators and consumers. While I appreciate thoughtful details, I understand the sweat and tears often spent on tweaking and revising.

Craftsmanship is beyond all praise, however, there are downsides of perfectionism, which a lot of creators can relate to.   

2. How can I improve it

Sure I can always make it better, but every additional minute spend at the moment is every minute I lost in my lifetime.

It is the effect of diminishing marginal return when I spend too much time on a task. 

Steps

1

Unplug

As the problem progress, it becomes necessary for me to set timers between different tasks. While high level of engagement is often desired, the decline of physical capacity is often less noticeable. Our mental capacity and physical capacity are correlated. A weakening physical condition will severely affect our mental health. Same rules apply when our mental toughness is weak, we feel powerless in some situations, etc. In order to avoid a mental drainage, it is necessary for me to schedule breaks in between tasks. Although sometimes I would intuitively to chase after the “flow” , I often find that it turn out to be a false sense of productiveness.

2

Creating Checkpoints

When I feel stuck, I would take notes on what needs to done and review later. Creating Checkpoints along the way helps me to offload my thoughts on paper rather than being troubled by them. It is necessary to over ride the influence of intensive mental engagement with the consideration of our physical capacity. Especially under the influence of coffee, time can easily disposed into endless cycle of repetitive revision.

3

Revision

Then go back later, and revise.
